The Authority Consultation:
The First 2 Minutes

Establishing Clinical Leadership and Eliminating Neediness
01 / The Pre-Frame

The success of your Report of Findings is determined in the first two minutes of your Consultation. If you enter the room as a "provider" seeking approval, you have already lost the case.

Authority is not demanded; it is signaled through Restraint and Certainty. You are not here to treat symptoms; you are here to evaluate the integrity of their nervous system.

The Identity Shift

Stop being a "Chiropractor" and start being an "Evaluator of Neurological Integrity." This shift changes your tone, your pacing, and your outcome.

The Expert Opening Script

"Mrs. Jones, before we begin, I want to be very clear about my goal today. I am not here to treat your symptoms or provide a temporary fix. My job is to evaluate the integrity of your nervous system and determine if your body has lost its ability to regulate itself. If I find a neurological problem, I will tell you. If I don't, I'll help you find someone who can. Does that make sense?"

02 / The Evaluator's Posture

No Chasing

An authority never chases. If you feel yourself trying to "convince" the patient of the value of chiropractic, you have stepped out of your authority and into sales. Back away.

Controlled Pacing

Move slower than the patient. Speak less than they do. Silence is your most powerful tool for establishing the clinical container.

"The patient is looking for a leader, not a friend. When you establish the rules of the environment early, they relax because they know they are in the hands of an expert."

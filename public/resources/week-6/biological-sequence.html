The Science of Visit Frequency

Why we recommend the visit schedule we do — explained simply.
The Key Concept

Patients often ask: 'Why do I need to come 3 times a week? Can not I just come once?' Here is how to explain it: Your body learns through repetition. When we adjust your spine, we are teaching it a new pattern. If we wait too long between adjustments, the old pattern comes back — like forgetting a new skill if you do not practice. Three times a week at the start gives your body enough repetition to hold the correction. Then we taper as your body gets stronger.

How to Use This

Phase 1 (3x/week): Breaking the old pattern. Your body has been stuck for months or years. It needs frequent input to change direction. Phase 2 (2x/week): Strengthening the new pattern. Your body is starting to hold the correction longer. We reduce frequency as it gets stronger. Phase 3 (1x/week): Maintaining the correction. Your body can now hold the adjustment for a full week. We are in maintenance mode.

For the Doctor: When patients push back on frequency, say: 'I understand that is a lot of visits. Here is my thinking — I would rather get this right in 12 weeks than have you come and go for 2 years without real change. The intensive phase is temporary. The results are permanent.'

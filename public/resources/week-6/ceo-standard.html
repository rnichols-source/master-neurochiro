How to shift from doing everything yourself to leading the practice.
The Shift

Most chiropractors are excellent doctors and terrible business owners. Not because they're bad at business — but because they never made the mental shift from "I do the work" to "I lead the people who do the work."

A CEO doesn't answer the phones, chase down no-shows, or restock supplies. A CEO does three things: sets the vision, builds the team, and watches the numbers.

Doctor Mode "I need to see more patients." "I need to get better at adjusting." "I need to work harder."
CEO Mode "Who can I train to handle this?" "What system would prevent this problem?" "Where are we losing money and why?"
The CEO Weekly Schedule
Monday: Review Numbers 15 minutes. Check last week's collections, new patients, visits, and retention. Are you on track for the monthly goal?
Tuesday: Team Check-In 10 minutes. Quick 1-on-1 with each team member. "What's working? What's not? What do you need from me?"
Wednesday: Systems 20 minutes. Pick one process that's broken or inconsistent and write a simple checklist for it.
Thursday: Patients Focus day. All clinical, no admin. This is your highest-value day — protect it.
Friday: Plan Next Week 15 minutes. Set 3 priorities for next week. Not 10. Three. What will move the practice forward most?

The Key Insight: You don't become a CEO by working more hours. You become a CEO by spending 1 hour per week on the business instead of in the business. Review numbers Monday. Check on your team Tuesday. Fix one system Wednesday. That's it — 45 minutes that change everything.

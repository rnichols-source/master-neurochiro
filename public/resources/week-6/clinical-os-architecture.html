Building Systems That
Run Without You

How to create repeatable processes so your practice grows even when you're not there.
The Test

Ask yourself: If you took a week off tomorrow, what would break? Whatever comes to mind first — that's the system you need to build next.

The 5 Systems Every Practice Needs
1. New Patient Flow From phone call → scheduling → Day 1 → Day 2 → Day 3 → onboarding. Every step should have a checklist. If your CA quits tomorrow, the next person should be able to follow the checklist and deliver the same experience.
2. Daily Huddle 5 minutes every morning. Review the schedule, highlight new patients and re-exams, check yesterday's missed visits. This one ritual keeps the entire team aligned.
3. Missed Visit Response Text within 4 hours, phone call on 2nd miss, doctor call on 3rd. Written out, posted on the wall, followed every time.
4. Payment Collection Collect at every visit. No exceptions. If there's a balance, address it at checkout — not 3 months later with a collections call. Your CA should know the exact script.
5. Weekly Numbers Review Every Monday: collections, new patients, visits, retention rate. Takes 15 minutes. If you're not tracking it, you can't improve it.

How to Build a System

Step 1: Do the task yourself and write down every step.
Step 2: Simplify to 3-5 steps max.
Step 3: Show someone else how to do it.
Step 4: Check their work for 2 weeks.
Step 5: It's theirs now.

The Key Insight: A system is just a checklist that someone follows the same way every time. You don't need software. You don't need a consultant. You need a piece of paper with 5 steps on it, posted where your team can see it. Start with one system this week. Add one more next week. In 5 weeks, your practice runs itself.

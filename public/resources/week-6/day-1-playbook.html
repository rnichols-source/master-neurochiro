Day 1: Your First Visit
With a New Patient

Exactly what to say and do in the first visit so the patient comes back for Day 2.
Your 4 Goals for Day 1
Goal 1 Make Them Feel Safe Tell them exactly what's going to happen today, step by step. Patients relax when they know what's coming.
Goal 2 Show Confidence Speak slowly. Don't rush. Your calm pace tells the patient you know what you're doing — more than any words can.
Goal 3 Collect Data — Don't Explain Yet Run your exams and scans without interpreting them out loud. Save the explanation for Day 2 — it'll have more impact.
Goal 4 End With a Clear Next Step The patient should leave knowing exactly when they're coming back and what will happen next.
What to Say — Step by Step

Step 1: The First 2 Minutes (Orientation)

Say This "Here's what we're going to do today. First, I'm going to listen to what's going on with you. Then we'll run a few specific tests. After that, I'll take the results home tonight and put together a clear picture of what's happening. Tomorrow, I'll walk you through everything and we'll talk about what we can do. Sound good?"

This takes 30 seconds and immediately lowers the patient's anxiety. They now know the entire sequence of the visit.

Step 2: Listen More Than You Talk

Ask them: "What's this preventing you from doing that matters most to you?" Then listen. Really listen. Don't jump in with a diagnosis. Their answer will become the anchor for your Day 2 presentation.

Step 3: Run Your Tests Quietly

Do your exam, take your scans, run your tests. Resist the urge to interpret results out loud. If you say "hmm, that's interesting" or start explaining findings, you dilute the impact of tomorrow's presentation.

Step 4: Close the Visit

Say This "I have everything I need. I'm going to analyze your results tonight and have a complete picture ready for you tomorrow at [time]. Don't change anything between now and then. I'll see you at [time]."

The Key Insight: Day 1 is not about teaching the patient anything. It's about making them feel safe, heard, and curious about what you'll share tomorrow. If they leave curious, you've done your job. If they leave overwhelmed, you've done too much.

Day 1 Checklist — Print This
  • Told them the visit sequence in the first 2 minutes
  • Asked "What's this preventing you from doing?"
  • Listened more than I talked
  • Did NOT interpret results out loud during the exam
  • Ended with a clear next step and time
  • Kept my pace slow and steady

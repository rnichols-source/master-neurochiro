Day 2: Presenting the
Care Plan

Exactly how to share your findings and recommend care so the patient says yes with confidence.
The 4 Parts of a Great Day 2
Part 1 Show What You Found Explain the problem in simple terms. Use their own words from Day 1. "Remember how you said you can't pick up your daughter without pain? Here's what's causing that."
Part 2 Explain What Happens If They Wait Help them understand the cost of doing nothing — not to scare them, but so they can make an informed choice. "If we leave this as-is, here's what typically happens over the next 6-12 months."
Part 3 Make Your Recommendation State it clearly: how often, for how long, and why. One recommendation — not three options. "Based on your results, I recommend 3 visits per week for the first 4 weeks, then we re-evaluate."
Part 4 Tell Them the Investment Say the number clearly. Don't apologize. Don't flinch. "The total investment for this plan is $3,600. We offer payment plans if that's easier — I'll have the team walk you through the options."
The Script — What to Actually Say
Opening (connect to their life) "Before I show you the results, I want to come back to what you told me yesterday. You said [their exact words from Day 1 — e.g., 'I can't play with my kids without hurting']. That's what we're solving today."
Presenting findings (keep it simple) "Here's what your scans show. [Point to one clear finding]. This tells me your nervous system is stuck in a stress pattern. That's why you're feeling [their symptom]. The good news is — this is something we can work with."
The recommendation (one plan, stated clearly) "Here's what I recommend: [X] visits per week for [Y] weeks. After that, we'll re-evaluate and adjust based on how you're responding. The total investment is $[amount]."
After you say the price (the most important part) Stop talking. Wait. Let them process. The silence is not awkward — it's respectful. They're making a decision. If you fill the silence with justifications, you signal that even YOU think it's too much.

The Key Insight: Patients don't say yes because they're excited. They say yes because they're certain — certain that you understand their problem, certain that you have a plan, and certain that you believe in it. Your confidence IS the close.

Day 2 Checklist — Print This
  • Connected findings to their own words from Day 1
  • Explained findings in simple, non-clinical language
  • Made ONE clear recommendation (not multiple options)
  • Stated the price clearly without apologizing
  • Stayed silent after giving the price
  • Did NOT over-explain or add extra justification

02 / Compliance Mechanics

Rhythm & Predictability

Predictable frequency builds a behavioral pattern. When the patient knows exactly what to expect at every visit, their stress drops and compliance increases.

Rhythm lowers stress. Structure makes behavior easier. When visits are predictable, the brain relaxes into the pattern.

The Drift Factor

Drift is subtle. It starts with a casual tone or a skipped onboarding conversation because the patient "seems to get it."

Erosion: Without Day 3, the authority established on Day 2 begins to bleed out. Skipping Day 3 leaves behavior unstable.

Structure vs. Motivation

Onboarding is the process of setting the behavioral rhythm for the entire care plan. Motivation fades. Structure remains. Day 3 onboarding exists not to resell, but to protect behavior.

"Structure makes behavior easier. Motivation fades. Structure remains. Day 3 exists not to convince, but to contain."

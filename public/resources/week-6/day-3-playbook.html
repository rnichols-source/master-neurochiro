Day 3: Setting Up the
Patient for Success

The onboarding visit that determines whether they complete their care plan or drop off at week 4.
Why Day 3 Matters More Than You Think

On Day 2, the patient agreed to a plan. On Day 3, they start following it. This is where most practices lose patients — not because the plan was wrong, but because nobody set clear expectations for what comes next.

Day 3 is not a treatment visit. It's an onboarding visit. Take 5 extra minutes to set up their entire experience.

Step 1 Confirm the Schedule Walk through their visit schedule for the next 4 weeks. Make sure they know exactly which days and times they're coming in.
Step 2 Set Re-Exam Expectations Tell them when you'll re-evaluate: "At visit 12, we'll re-scan and compare to your Day 1 results."
Step 3 Prepare Them for Ups and Downs Let them know that progress isn't always linear. Some weeks they'll feel great, some they won't. That's normal.
What to Say on Day 3
The Onboarding Script "Before we get started today, I want to make sure you know exactly what the next few weeks look like. You'll be coming in [3 times a week / Monday, Wednesday, Friday] for the first 4 weeks. At visit 12, we'll do a full re-evaluation — same scans as Day 1 — so we can measure exactly how you're responding. Some days you'll feel great. Some days you might feel a little sore. That's completely normal and actually a good sign. Any questions before we begin?"

Why This Prevents Drop-Off

Without Day 3 onboarding: The patient comes in for a few visits, starts feeling better around visit 6, and thinks "I'm good now." They cancel. You lose $2,000-$4,000.

With Day 3 onboarding: The patient knows the re-exam is at visit 12. They have a milestone to work toward. When they feel better at visit 6, they think "I'm halfway there" instead of "I'm done."

The Key Insight: Patients don't drop off because they don't like you. They drop off because they don't know what comes next. Day 3 removes that uncertainty. Give them a schedule, a milestone, and realistic expectations — and they'll stick with the plan.

Day 3 Checklist — Print This
  • Confirmed their visit schedule for the next 4 weeks
  • Told them when their first re-evaluation is
  • Warned them about normal ups and downs
  • Asked if they have any questions
  • Did NOT try to re-sell or re-justify the plan
  • Treated this as onboarding, not just another visit

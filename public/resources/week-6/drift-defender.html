Handling No-Shows
and Missed Visits

What to say when a patient misses a visit — and how to get them back without being awkward.
Why Patients Miss Visits

A missed visit is rarely about you. It's usually one of three things:

1. Life happened. Kid got sick, work meeting, forgot.

2. They're feeling better and subconsciously deprioritized the visit.

3. They're having doubts about whether to continue.

The key is to respond quickly and without judgment. The longer you wait, the harder it is for them to come back.

The Rule Contact them within 24 hours of the missed visit. Not 3 days later. Not next week. The same day or next morning.
Who Should Call Your front desk or CA — not you. It should feel like a check-in, not a guilt trip from the doctor.
Scripts for Each Situation

First Missed Visit (Phone or Text)

Text Script "Hi [Name]! We missed you today. Hope everything is OK. Want me to reschedule you for tomorrow or later this week? Just reply with a time that works. 😊"

Second Missed Visit (Needs a Call)

Phone Script "Hey [Name], this is [CA name] from [Clinic]. I noticed you've missed your last couple of visits and just wanted to make sure everything is OK. Dr. [Your Name] wanted me to check in — is there anything we can help with? We have a few spots open this week if you'd like to get back on track."

Third Missed Visit (Doctor's Call)

Doctor Phone Script "[Name], it's Dr. [Your Name]. I just wanted to call personally because I noticed you haven't been in for a few visits. I'm not calling to pressure you — I just want to make sure you're doing OK and that I haven't missed anything on my end. If something's changed or you have questions about the plan, I'd rather talk about it than have you disappear. Can we chat for a minute?"

The Key Insight: The first no-show is a scheduling issue. The second is a warning sign. The third means you're losing them. Act fast, act warm, and never make them feel guilty. The goal is to make coming back easy — not to punish them for leaving.

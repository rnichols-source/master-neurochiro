What to Expect During Care

A patient handout explaining the normal ups and downs of chiropractic recovery.
The Key Concept

Getting better is not a straight line. Most patients expect to feel a little better every single day. The reality is: you will have good days and not-so-good days, especially in the first few weeks. This is completely normal. Your body is reorganizing patterns it has been stuck in for months or years. That process has ups and downs.

How to Use This

Week 1-2: You may feel a little sore after adjustments. This is normal — like being sore after your first workout. Week 3-4: Most patients start feeling noticeably better. Pain decreases, energy increases. This is where the temptation to quit starts — 'I feel fine, do I really need to keep coming?' Week 5-8: Progress may plateau for a bit. Do not panic — your body is stabilizing. The real structural changes are happening underneath. Week 9-12: Re-exam time. This is where we compare scans and see the objective improvement that your body has been building toward.

For the Doctor: Print this page and hand it to every new patient on Day 3. It prevents the #1 reason patients quit early: they expect linear progress and get frustrated when it does not happen. If they know the pattern in advance, they will trust the process.

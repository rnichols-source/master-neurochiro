Making Money When

How to build a practice that generates income without requiring you in the room every hour.
The Freedom Question

Ask yourself: How much does your practice make when you take a day off? If the answer is zero, your practice isn't a business — it's a job. Here's how to change that.

3 Stages of Practice Freedom
Stage 1: You Are the Practice You see every patient. You answer every call. You make every decision. Revenue = your hours × your rate. If you're here, this is normal — but it's not sustainable.
Stage 2: You Lead the Practice You have a trained team handling scheduling, payments, follow-up, and room prep. You focus only on adjusting and presenting. You could take a Friday off and things would run.
Stage 3: The Practice Runs Without You You have an associate or partner seeing patients. Systems run the operations. You could take a week off and collections stay steady. This is the goal — but it requires Stages 1 and 2 first.

Where Are You Now?

Be honest. Most solo chiropractors are in Stage 1. The goal of this program is to get you solidly into Stage 2 within 8 weeks. Stage 3 is a longer-term play that requires hiring an associate — which the Council program covers.

Your First Step Toward Freedom

Track Your "CEO Hours"

This week, track how many hours you spend on tasks someone else could do: answering phones, scheduling, ordering supplies, cleaning, billing. Write it down. That number is your freedom gap.

Delegate One Thing

Pick the easiest task from your list. Write a 5-step checklist. Train someone on it this week. One task delegated per week = 12 tasks off your plate in 3 months. That's an entire workday freed up.

The Key Insight: Freedom isn't about working less — it's about working on the right things. The right things are: seeing patients, presenting care plans, and leading your team. Everything else should be systematized and handed off. Start with one task this week.

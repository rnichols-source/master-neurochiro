How to Build
a Care Plan

A simple framework for choosing visit frequency and plan length based on patient findings.
The 3 Phases — Use This for Every Patient
Phase 1: Stabilize (Weeks 1-4) 3x per week The body needs frequent input to break old patterns. This is where you address the acute issues and establish a new baseline. Think of it as laying the foundation.
Phase 2: Strengthen (Weeks 5-8) 2x per week The initial problem is stabilizing. Now you're training the body to hold the correction on its own. Like building muscle — it takes consistent reps over weeks.
Phase 3: Maintain (Weeks 9-12) 1x per week The correction is holding. Now you're making sure life stress (work, sitting, poor sleep) doesn't pull them back. Re-evaluate at the end of this phase.

How to Choose the Right Plan Length

Use your dysfunction score (from the Clinical Scoring tool) as your guide:

Mild (Score: 0-25%) 24 visits over 8-10 weeks. Start at 3x/week, taper to 1x/week.
Moderate (Score: 25-50%) 36 visits over 12 weeks. Full 3-phase plan as described.
Significant (Score: 50-75%) 48 visits over 14-16 weeks. Extended Phase 1 (6 weeks at 3x).
Severe (Score: 75%+) 60+ visits. Extended all phases. Consider more frequent re-evaluations.
How to Present It
Say This "Based on your results, here's what I recommend: 3 visits per week for the first 4 weeks to stabilize, then 2 per week for a month to strengthen, then 1 per week for a month to make sure it holds. At the end, we'll re-scan and compare to today. If you're ahead of schedule, we adjust. If you need more time, we'll know exactly why."

Why this works: It gives them a clear timeline, a reason for each phase, and a built-in re-evaluation. They know what to expect and when they'll see progress.

The Key Insight: Don't let the patient's budget determine the plan. Let the findings determine the plan, then help them find a way to afford it. If you recommend fewer visits than they need because you're worried about the price, you're not being affordable — you're being ineffective.

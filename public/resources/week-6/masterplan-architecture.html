How to Build a Care Plan

A practical guide to choosing visit frequency and plan length
01 / Why Repetition Matters

Getting better is not a one-time event — it's a gradual process. As BJ Palmer and Gonstead understood, the "set" of the segment is only as permanent as the stability of the nervous system that controls it.

Care plan frequency is not a financial decision—it is a biological requirement. You are not "treating" a spine; you are retraining a neural pattern through repeated, consistent input.

Why Consistency Matters Neurological change requires consistent spacing to prevent the system from reverting to dominant stress-biased patterns.
Building Momentum The cumulative effect of repeated adjustments creates the "tipping point" where the new pattern becomes the baseline.
02 / The Three Phases of Care

01 / Phase 1: Stabilize

High frequency (3x/week) to break the existing neurological momentum. This is where we stabilize the "leaks" in the system and establish a new tone.

02 / Phase 2: Strengthen

Moderate frequency (2x/week) to strengthen the new pattern. This is the "Gym" phase of chiropractic, where neurological endurance is built.

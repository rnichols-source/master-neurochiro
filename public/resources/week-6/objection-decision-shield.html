When Others
Question the Plan

What to say BEFORE the patient goes home — so their spouse, their MD, or Google doesn't talk them out of care.
The Problem

The patient says yes. They commit to the plan. They leave your office feeling confident. Then they go home and tell their spouse, call their doctor, or Google "is chiropractic worth it?"

Within 24 hours, their commitment is gone. Not because your plan was wrong — but because someone who has never seen their scans or heard their story gave an uninformed opinion.

The fix is simple: bring it up before they leave. Don't wait for the objection to happen at home. Address it in the room while they're still with you.

This isn't manipulation — it's preparation. You're equipping them with the information they need to make their own decision, even when others push back.

3 Scripts — Use Before They Leave

1. The Spouse Conversation

Say This Before They Walk Out "When you share this with your spouse tonight, they're going to have questions — and that's totally normal. They haven't seen your scans or heard me explain the findings, so they're working with incomplete information. If it would help, I'm happy to have them come to your next visit so I can walk them through everything too. Would that be useful?"

This does two things: it validates the spouse's opinion, and it positions you as open and transparent — not defensive.

2. The "My Doctor Said..." Response

Say This "If your medical doctor has thoughts about this, that's great — they're looking out for you. Just keep in mind that what we're addressing here is different from what they typically manage. They focus on symptoms and medication. We're focused on how your nervous system is functioning. Both are important, but they're solving different problems."

This doesn't criticize the MD — it simply clarifies that the two approaches are different, not competing.

3. The Google / Internet Research

Say This "You might look some of this up online tonight — and that's totally fine. Just remember that Google doesn't know your specific scan results or your health history. What I shared with you today is based on YOUR data, not a general article. If you read something that concerns you, bring it to your next visit and we'll talk about it."

The Key Insight: Don't wait for the patient to come back and say "my husband thinks I should try physical therapy first." Address it proactively, in the room, before they leave. The 30 seconds it takes to say one of these scripts can save a $3,000-$5,000 case.

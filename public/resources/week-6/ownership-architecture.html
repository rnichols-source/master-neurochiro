Delegating Without
Losing Control

How to hand off tasks to your team without worrying that things will fall apart.
The Real Reason You Do Everything Yourself

Most clinic owners don't delegate because they tried once, it went badly, and they decided "it's faster if I just do it myself." That's true in the short term. But in the long term, it means you can never take a day off, you're exhausted, and your team never grows.

The fix isn't trusting your team more. It's giving them better instructions.

The Delegation Formula
Step 1: Write It Down If you can't write the task in 3-5 steps, you don't understand it well enough to delegate it. Write the steps first — then hand them the written steps, not a verbal explanation.
Step 2: Show Them Once Do the task in front of them while they watch. Then have them do it while you watch. Then let them do it alone. Three reps — not one and done.
Step 3: Check, Don't Hover Set a check-in point (daily for the first week, weekly after that). Review the outcome, not the process. If the result is right, don't nitpick how they got there.
Step 4: Let Them Own It Once they can do it consistently for 2 weeks, it's theirs. Stop checking. If something goes wrong, they come to you — you don't go to them.
What to Delegate First

Delegate These

• Scheduling and rescheduling
• Payment collection at checkout
• Missed visit follow-up calls/texts
• Room prep and cleanup
• Supply ordering

Keep These (For Now)

• New patient phone calls (until they have the script down)
• Re-exam presentations
• Care plan recommendations
• Difficult patient conversations
• Financial hardship cases

The Key Insight: Delegation isn't about trust — it's about systems. If you give someone a clear checklist, they'll follow it. If you give them vague instructions, they'll guess. The problem is almost never the person — it's the quality of the instructions you gave them.

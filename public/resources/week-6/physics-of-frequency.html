How Long Does Each Phase Take?

A practical guide to timing your care plan phases.
The Key Concept

This is the most common question from both doctors and patients: how long should each phase last? Here is a simple framework based on dysfunction severity. Mild (Score 0-25%): 4 weeks at 3x, 4 weeks at 2x, 4 weeks at 1x = 24 visits total. Moderate (25-50%): 6 weeks at 3x, 4 weeks at 2x, 4 weeks at 1x = 36 visits. Significant (50-75%): 8 weeks at 3x, 4 weeks at 2x, 4 weeks at 1x = 48 visits. Severe (75%+): 10+ weeks at 3x, extended taper = 60+ visits.

How to Use This

These are guidelines, not rules. Re-evaluate at the end of each phase. If the patient is responding faster, taper sooner. If progress is slow, extend the phase. The dysfunction score from your Clinical Scoring tool gives you a starting point — then adjust based on what you actually see.

For the Doctor: Keep the math simple. When presenting to patients, round to the nearest month: 'About 3 months of care' is easier to process than '36 visits over 12 weeks at varying frequencies.' Give them the big picture, save the details for the schedule.'

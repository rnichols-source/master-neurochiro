3 ways to increase income with the patients you already have — without working more hours.
The Math Most Doctors Miss

Most chiropractors think the answer to more revenue is more new patients. But getting a new patient costs 5-10x more than keeping one. The fastest path to higher revenue is increasing the value of every patient you already see.

There are only 3 ways to grow revenue:

1. See more patients (hard, expensive)
2. Charge more per visit (simple, immediate)
3. Keep patients longer (highest long-term impact)

Quick Example If you see 100 visits/week at $55/visit = $22,000/month. Raise to $65/visit = $26,000/month. That's $48,000/year more — with zero extra work, zero new patients, zero marketing spend.
3 Levers to Pull This Month
Lever 1: Raise Your Per-Visit Fee by $10 Most practices are undercharging. A $10 increase across 400 monthly visits = $4,000/month extra. Patients rarely notice or complain about a $10 change. Use the Break-Even Calculator to see the impact.
Lever 2: Improve Case Acceptance by 10% If you're at 60% acceptance and move to 70%, that's 1-2 extra patients per month starting care. At $3,000 average case value, that's $3,000-$6,000/month. Use the Day 2 scripts.
Lever 3: Reduce Drop-Off by 20% If 5 patients quit early each month and you save 1 of them, that's $3,000-$5,000 in recovered revenue. Use the Retention and Day 3 guides to address this.

The Compound Effect

Pull all 3 levers at once: raise fees by $10, improve acceptance by 10%, reduce drop-off by 20%. Combined impact: $8,000-$15,000/month in additional revenue without a single new patient.

The Key Insight: You don't need more patients. You need more value per patient. A practice seeing 100 visits/week at $85/visit with 80% retention will always outperform a practice seeing 150 visits/week at $50/visit with 50% retention. Focus on value and retention first. Growth follows.

01 / The Transition

Patients drift away because no one told them what comes after "feeling better." If you avoid this conversation, the patient fills in the blank with: "I guess I'm done."

Ongoing care is not something you push or avoid; it is something you explain. It is the logical progression of health optimization.

Standard Analogies
The Gym: You don't get in shape and then never work out again.
The Dentist: You don't wait for a cavity to brush your teeth.
Conditioning: Athletes train to maintain their level, not just to reach it.
02 / Two Phases of Care

Correction

Actively working to change a neurological pattern. This phase has a beginning, a direction, and a specific clinical goal of reorganization.

Protection

Defending the progress already made. Life continues to apply stress—work, gravity, lack of sleep. Protection keeps the system sharp.

Population Behavior

Some will stay, some will go. Your job is to provide the logic. If you push, they resist. If you avoid, they drift. If you explain calmly and logically, they decide with clarity. Hold your structure without apology.

"Correction is about change. Protection is about certainty. Transitioning from one to the other is the mark of a master communicator."

The Power of
Saying Less

How to use silence and a steady voice to close more cases — without feeling salesy.
The Problem: Talking Too Much

Most chiropractors talk too much during their care plan presentation. Not because they don't know what they're doing — but because silence feels uncomfortable.

When a patient goes quiet after hearing the price, the natural instinct is to fill the space: "Of course, we also have payment plans..." or "If that's too many visits, we could do..." Every word you add weakens your recommendation.

The rule is simple: Say what you need to say. Then stop.

The 5-Second Rule After you state the price or make your recommendation, count to 5 in your head before saying anything else. Let them process. The silence isn't awkward — it's respectful.
The 60/40 Rule The patient should be talking 60% of the time, you 40%. If you're talking more than the patient, you're explaining too much.
How Your Voice Affects Their Decision

Keep Your Voice Steady

When you say the price, keep the same tone as when you explained the findings. Don't speed up. Don't lower your volume. Don't let your voice go up at the end like a question.

Steady voice = "I believe in this recommendation."

Shaky voice = "I'm hoping you'll say yes."

Patients hear the difference immediately.

Don't Over-Explain

If you've already explained the findings and the plan, you don't need to re-explain when you say the price. Adding justifications after the number signals that even YOU think it's too much.

Don't say this "The investment is $3,600... but of course that includes all the visits and the re-exam and the home exercises and the scan comparison and..."
Say this instead "The total investment for this plan is $3,600. We have payment options if that's easier. [Then stop.]"

The Key Insight: The less you say after the recommendation, the more confident you appear. Patients don't need more information — they need to see that YOU are certain. Your calm silence tells them everything they need to know.

Quick Reference — Tape This to Your Desk
Before the Presentation Take a breath. Slow down. Remind yourself: "I am presenting their results, not selling a product."
After Saying the Price Stop talking. Count to 5. Let them respond first. If they say nothing, ask: "What questions do you have?"

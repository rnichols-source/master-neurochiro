What to say when a patient says "I feel better" and wants to stop care early.
The #1 Reason Patients Leave Early

Around visit 6-8, something predictable happens: the patient starts feeling better. The pain that brought them in is 60-70% gone. And they think: "Do I really need to keep coming?"

This is the most dangerous moment in any care plan. If you haven't set expectations, you'll lose them here.

The Cost of Early Drop-Off Losing 5 patients/month at visit 8 of a 36-visit plan = ~$9,000/month in lost revenue. That's $109,000/year from one preventable problem.
3 Conversations That Prevent Drop-Off

1. Day 3: Set the Expectation Early

Say This During Onboarding "Around week 3, you're going to start feeling a lot better. That's great — it means the plan is working. But feeling better and being better are two different things. The re-exam at visit [12] will show us if the problem is actually correcting. That's when we'll know for sure."

2. Visit 6-8: Reinforce When They Improve

Say This When They Mention Improvement "I'm glad you're feeling better — that tells me your body is responding. Remember, the re-exam is at visit [12]. That's when we compare scans and see the real structural changes. We're on track."

3. Re-Exam: Show the Proof

Say This When Showing Progress "Here's your Day 1 scan. Here's today. [Point to the change.] This is why you're feeling better — your body is actually changing. We're [X]% through the plan. Let's finish strong."

The Key Insight: Patients don't leave because they're unhappy. They leave because "feeling better" feels like "problem solved." Separate the two — early and often. Set the expectation on Day 3, reinforce at visit 6-8, prove it at the re-exam.

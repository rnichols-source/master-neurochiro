The Complete
Patient Sequence

Print this. Tape it to your wall. Follow it with every patient.
Day 1 — First Visit
Step 1Orient (First 2 Minutes)Tell them exactly what today looks like. "First I'll listen, then we'll test, then I'll analyze tonight."
Step 2ListenAsk: "What's this preventing you from doing?" Then be quiet.
Step 3Collect Data SilentlyRun exams and scans. Don't interpret out loud.
Step 4Close"I'll have your results ready tomorrow at [time]."
Day 2 — Care Plan Presentation
Step 1Connect to Their LifeUse their exact words from Day 1. "You said you can't..."
Step 2Show Findings SimplyOne key finding. Plain language. No jargon.
Step 3RecommendOne plan. "[X] visits per week for [Y] weeks."
Step 4State the Price"The total investment is $[amount]." Then stop talking.
Day 3 — Onboarding
Step 1Confirm ScheduleWalk through their visit days for the next 4 weeks.
Step 2Set Re-Exam"At visit [12], we'll compare scans to Day 1."
Step 3Prepare for Ups & Downs"Some weeks you'll feel great, some you won't. That's normal."
Visit 6-8 — When They Feel Better
SayReinforce Progress"Glad you're feeling better — the re-exam at visit [12] will show us the structural changes."
Re-Exam — Show the Proof
SayCompare Scans"Here's Day 1. Here's today. This is why you're feeling better."
End of Plan — Transition
SayOngoing Care"We fixed the problem. Now we keep it fixed. Most patients choose [1x/week or 2x/month]."
If They Hesitate
SayAsk the Real Question"Is it the plan or the investment? I want to make sure I've answered your questions."

Print this page and tape it inside a cabinet door in your adjusting room. Before every new patient, glance at it. Within 4 weeks, you won't need it anymore — but for now, let the sequence guide you.

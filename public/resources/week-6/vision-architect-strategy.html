Your 10-Year Vision

5 questions to answer now that determine where your practice ends up in a decade.
Why This Matters

Most chiropractors plan week to week. The ones who build something lasting plan year to year. These 5 questions take 20 minutes to answer — and they'll shape every decision you make from here forward.

Answer These 5 Questions (Write Your Answers Down)
1. What does your ideal week look like in 10 years? How many days do you work? How many patients do you see? Are you adjusting every patient yourself, or do you have associates? Are you teaching, speaking, mentoring? Be specific about the hours and the lifestyle.
2. What is your target annual income? Not "more." A number. $200K? $500K? $1M? Write it down. Then work backward: at your current per-visit rate, how many visits per year does that require? Is that realistic with one location, or do you need to grow?
3. Do you want to own one practice or multiple? There's no wrong answer. One amazing practice with a great team can generate $500K+/year. Multiple locations can scale further but require a completely different skillset (management, not clinical). Know which path you want before you start walking.
4. What will you be known for? In 10 years, when someone in your community says your name, what do they say next? "The best chiropractor in town"? "The doctor who specializes in pediatric care"? "The practice that changed my life"? Your reputation is your most valuable asset — decide what it will be.
5. What needs to be true in 2 years to be on track? This is the most important question. If your 10-year vision requires $500K revenue and you're at $200K now, what does year 2 look like? Maybe it's $300K with one staff member and 80% retention. Make this milestone specific and measurable.

The Key Insight: Your 10-year vision isn't a goal — it's a filter. Once you know where you're going, every decision becomes easier: "Does this hire get me closer or further?" "Does this marketing spend make sense for the practice I'm building?" "Am I solving a today problem or building for tomorrow?" The vision answers these automatically.

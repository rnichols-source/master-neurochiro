Chiropractor's Guide — Read Before Using

When to Use This Agreement

  • You are hiring a new associate doctor as a W-2 employee of your chiropractic practice.
  • You are converting an existing independent contractor to employee status and need a formal written agreement.
  • You are restructuring an existing associate relationship that currently has no written contract or only a handshake deal.
  • You are onboarding a recent graduate from chiropractic college and want clear expectations documented from day one.

What to Customize vs. Keep Standard

  • Always customize: Compensation figures, bonus thresholds, non-compete radius and duration, PTO allowances, CE stipend amounts, work schedule, and any state-specific language required by your jurisdiction.
  • Keep standard: Confidentiality provisions, dispute resolution mechanisms, general provisions (severability, entire agreement, amendments), and intellectual property ownership clauses. These are battle-tested provisions that protect both parties.
  • Fill in every blank field before signing. Unfilled blanks create ambiguity and invite disputes.

Common Mistakes That Cost Practice Owners Thousands

  • No written agreement at all. Verbal agreements are nearly impossible to enforce. If the relationship sours, you have no protection for your patient base, your trade secrets, or your investment in training the associate.
  • Vague compensation language. "We'll work something out" is not a compensation plan. Specify exact base salary, exact bonus percentages, exact collection thresholds, and exact payment timing. Ambiguity breeds resentment and lawsuits.
  • Overly aggressive non-compete. A 50-mile, 5-year non-compete will be thrown out by almost every court in America. Keep it reasonable: 10-25 miles, 1-2 years. An enforceable narrow restriction is worth infinitely more than an unenforceable broad one.
  • No termination notice period. If an associate can walk out on Friday and open across the street on Monday, you lose patients. Require 60-90 days written notice to allow for proper transition of care.
  • Failing to address tail coverage. If the associate leaves and you have not addressed who pays for malpractice tail coverage, you may be exposed to claims filed after departure.
  • Not specifying what happens to patient records. Patient records belong to the practice, not the associate. Make this explicit.

3. Position and Duties

3.1 Position. Associate shall serve as an Associate Doctor of Chiropractic within the Practice. Associate shall report directly to the Managing Doctor identified in Section 1 or such other supervising doctor as Employer may designate from time to time.

3.2 Clinical Duties. Associate's clinical duties shall include, but are not limited to:

  6. Providing patient education on wellness, exercise, nutrition, and ergonomics;
  7. Making appropriate referrals to other healthcare providers when clinical indications warrant;
  8. Performing re-examinations at intervals consistent with the Practice's clinical protocols and payer requirements; and

3.3 Documentation. Associate shall maintain thorough, accurate, and timely clinical documentation for all patient encounters, including but not limited to:

  2. Initial examination reports, re-examination reports, and discharge summaries;
  3. Treatment plans with measurable goals, frequency recommendations, and anticipated duration;
  4. Informed consent documentation;
  5. All documentation required for proper billing and coding compliance; and
  6. Compliance with all federal, state, and local documentation requirements, including HIPAA.

3.5 Professional Conduct. Associate shall at all times:

  1. Maintain an active, unrestricted license to practice chiropractic in the applicable state;
  2. Comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, rules, and regulations governing the practice of chiropractic;
  3. Adhere to the ethical standards established by the American Chiropractic Association and the applicable state board;
  4. Follow all written policies, procedures, and clinical protocols of the Practice;
  5. Represent the Practice in a professional and courteous manner to patients, staff, referral sources, and the community;
  6. Refrain from any conduct that could adversely affect the reputation or goodwill of the Practice; and
  7. Promptly notify Employer of any disciplinary action, complaint, investigation, or malpractice claim involving Associate.

3.6 Exclusivity. During the Term of this Agreement, Associate shall devote Associate's full professional time, attention, and energies to the Practice and shall not engage in the practice of chiropractic outside of the Practice without Employer's prior written consent, except for volunteer or charitable work that does not interfere with Associate's duties hereunder.

7. Non-Competition Covenant

7.1 Non-Competition. During the Term of this Agreement and for a period of One (1) Year / Two (2) Years following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, regardless of the reason for termination (the "Restricted Period"), Associate shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in any of the following activities within a __________ (e.g., 15)-mile radius of the Practice's primary location at Practice Address, or any satellite location at which Associate provided services during the twelve (12) months preceding termination (the "Restricted Area"):

  1. Own, operate, manage, or control a chiropractic practice or clinic;
  2. Serve as an employee, independent contractor, partner, officer, director, consultant, or agent of any chiropractic practice or clinic;
  3. Solicit, treat, or provide chiropractic services to any patient of the Practice; or
  4. Assist any person or entity in establishing or operating a chiropractic practice or clinic that competes with the Practice.

7.2 Permitted Activities. Notwithstanding Section 7.1, the following activities shall not constitute a violation of this non-competition covenant:

  1. Practicing chiropractic at a location outside the Restricted Area;
  2. Teaching or lecturing at an accredited educational institution;
  3. Providing non-clinical consulting services that do not involve the direct treatment of patients;
  4. Performing volunteer chiropractic services at community health events or charitable organizations; or
  5. Employment in a healthcare field other than chiropractic (e.g., acupuncture, physical therapy, if separately licensed).

7.3 Reasonableness. Associate acknowledges and agrees that the restrictions set forth in this Section 7 are reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic area and are necessary to protect Employer's legitimate business interests, including the Practice's patient relationships, goodwill, confidential information, and investment in Associate's training and professional development. Associate further acknowledges that Associate has had the opportunity to consult with independent legal counsel regarding these restrictions.

7.4 Survival. The obligations set forth in this Section 7 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement in accordance with their terms, regardless of whether the termination is with or without cause, voluntary or involuntary.

8. Non-Solicitation of Patients

8.1 Patient Non-Solicitation. During the Term and for a period of __________ (e.g., 24) months following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, Associate shall not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Contact, solicit, or communicate with any patient of the Practice for the purpose of providing chiropractic services or inducing such patient to transfer care to Associate or any other provider;
  2. Accept or treat any patient of the Practice who seeks care from Associate, unless the patient independently locates Associate without any solicitation and the treatment occurs outside the Restricted Area;
  3. Use any patient list, contact information, medical records, or other patient data obtained during employment with the Practice for the purpose of soliciting patients; or
  4. Disparage the Practice or its providers to any patient, referral source, or member of the community.

8.2 Definition of "Patient of the Practice." For purposes of this Section 8, a "Patient of the Practice" means any individual who received chiropractic services at the Practice at any time during the twenty-four (24) months preceding the date of Associate's termination, as well as any prospective patient with whom Associate or the Practice had an active consultation or scheduled appointment at the time of termination.

8.3 Patient Notification. Upon termination of this Agreement, Employer shall be solely responsible for notifying patients of Associate's departure and facilitating the transition of care. Associate shall not independently contact patients regarding Associate's departure.

9. Non-Solicitation of Employees

9.1 Employee Non-Solicitation. During the Term and for a period of __________ (e.g., 12) months following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, Associate shall not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Recruit, solicit, hire, or attempt to hire any employee, independent contractor, or agent of the Practice;
  2. Encourage or induce any employee, independent contractor, or agent of the Practice to terminate their relationship with the Practice; or
  3. Assist any person or entity in recruiting or soliciting any employee, independent contractor, or agent of the Practice.

9.2 Scope. This restriction applies to any individual who is employed by or providing services to the Practice at the time of Associate's termination, or who was employed by or providing services to the Practice at any time during the six (6) months preceding Associate's termination.

10. Confidentiality

10.1 Confidential Information Defined. "Confidential Information" means all non-public information relating to the Practice's business, operations, patients, and proprietary methods, including but not limited to:

  1. Patient records, contact information, treatment histories, and billing records;
  2. Patient lists, referral source lists, and marketing databases;
  3. Financial information, including revenue, expenses, profitability, fee schedules, and payer contracts;
  4. Business strategies, expansion plans, and marketing plans;
  5. Clinical protocols, treatment methodologies, case management systems, and proprietary techniques;
  6. Employee compensation information, staffing plans, and organizational charts;
  7. Software systems, technology platforms, and digital assets;
  8. Vendor relationships, supply agreements, and pricing arrangements; and
  9. Any other information designated by Employer as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

10.2 Obligations. Associate agrees to:

  1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to any third party without Employer's prior written consent;
  2. Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing Associate's duties under this Agreement;
  3. Take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information;
  4. Promptly return all documents, files, electronic records, and materials containing Confidential Information upon termination of employment or upon Employer's request; and
  5. Comply with all applicable federal and state laws regarding the confidentiality of patient health information, including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA).

10.3 Exceptions. The obligations set forth in this Section 10 shall not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Associate; (b) is independently developed by Associate without use of Confidential Information; (c) is disclosed pursuant to a valid court order or subpoena, provided Associate gives Employer prompt written notice and cooperates with Employer in seeking a protective order; or (d) is required to be disclosed by applicable law or regulation.

10.4 Duration. The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Section 10 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement and shall continue in perpetuity with respect to trade secrets and for a period of five (5) years with respect to all other Confidential Information.

11. Intellectual Property

11.1 Practice Property. Associate acknowledges and agrees that the following constitute the exclusive property of Employer and shall remain so at all times during and after the Term of this Agreement:

  1. All patient records, files, charts, and documentation (whether in physical or electronic form);
  2. Patient lists, referral source databases, and all patient contact information;
  3. Marketing materials, advertisements, brochures, website content, and social media accounts created for or on behalf of the Practice;
  4. Clinical protocols, treatment templates, care plan formats, and documentation systems developed by or for the Practice;
  5. Training materials, onboarding guides, and standard operating procedures;
  6. The Practice's name, logo, trademarks, service marks, and trade dress; and
  7. Any work product, inventions, or materials created by Associate within the scope of Associate's employment or using the Practice's resources.

11.2 Assignment. To the extent any intellectual property rights vest in Associate by operation of law, Associate hereby irrevocably assigns to Employer all right, title, and interest in and to such intellectual property. Associate agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably necessary to effectuate this assignment.

11.3 No Removal. Upon termination of this Agreement, Associate shall immediately return to Employer all property of the Practice, including but not limited to keys, access cards, equipment, patient records, electronic files, copies of Confidential Information, and all items listed in Section 11.1. Associate shall not retain any copies, summaries, or excerpts thereof in any format.

12. Termination

12.1 Termination by Employer for Cause. Employer may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to Associate for any of the following reasons (each constituting "Cause"):

  1. Revocation, suspension, or restriction of Associate's license to practice chiropractic;
  2. Conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, a felony or any crime involving moral turpitude, dishonesty, or fraud;
  3. Commission of an act of fraud, embezzlement, theft, or misappropriation against the Practice;
  4. Material breach of any provision of this Agreement, including but not limited to confidentiality, non-compete, or professional conduct obligations, that is not cured within fifteen (15) days after written notice from Employer;
  5. Chronic or repeated failure to perform clinical duties in a competent and professional manner after written warning;
  6. Substantiated patient abuse, neglect, or sexual misconduct;
  7. Willful violation of federal or state healthcare regulations, including billing fraud or HIPAA violations;
  8. Positive drug test for illegal substances or impairment by drugs or alcohol while on duty;
  9. Unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information; or
  10. Any conduct that materially damages the reputation or goodwill of the Practice.

12.2 Termination by Employer Without Cause. Employer may terminate this Agreement without Cause by providing Associate with sixty (60) / ninety (90) days' prior written notice. During the notice period, Associate shall continue to perform all duties and facilitate the orderly transition of patient care.

12.3 Termination by Associate. Associate may terminate this Agreement by providing Employer with sixty (60) / ninety (90) days' prior written notice. During the notice period, Associate shall continue to perform all duties and assist in the transition of care to another provider designated by Employer.

12.4 Termination Upon Death or Disability. This Agreement shall terminate automatically upon Associate's death. If Associate becomes permanently disabled and is unable to perform the essential functions of Associate's position for a period of ninety (90) / one hundred twenty (120) consecutive days, Employer may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

12.5 Effect of Termination on Restrictive Covenants. The non-competition, non-solicitation, confidentiality, and intellectual property provisions set forth in Sections 7 through 11 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement regardless of the reason for termination, including termination by Employer without Cause, unless otherwise agreed in a separate written release executed by both Parties.

12.6 Final Compensation. Upon termination, Employer shall pay Associate all earned but unpaid Base Salary through the effective date of termination, any accrued but unused PTO (if applicable per Section 5.3), and any Production Bonus earned but not yet paid, calculated through the date of termination. Such amounts shall be paid within the time period required by applicable state law or, if no specific time period is mandated, within thirty (30) days of the termination date.

13. Malpractice Insurance

13.1 Coverage During Employment. As set forth in Section 5.5, Employer shall maintain professional liability insurance covering Associate during the Term of this Agreement. Associate shall cooperate fully with the insurer in the investigation and defense of any claim.

13.2 Tail Coverage. If the malpractice insurance policy maintained by Employer is a "claims-made" policy, tail coverage (also known as an extended reporting period endorsement) shall be required upon termination of Associate's employment. The cost of tail coverage shall be allocated as follows:

  1. Termination by Employer without Cause: Employer shall pay one hundred percent (100%) of the cost of tail coverage.
  2. Termination by Employer for Cause: Associate shall pay one hundred percent (100%) of the cost of tail coverage.
  3. Voluntary resignation by Associate: Describe allocation, e.g., "Associate shall pay 100%" or "Parties shall split equally" or "Employer pays if Associate has been employed for 3+ years".
  4. Mutual termination or non-renewal: The Parties shall split the cost of tail coverage equally (50/50).

13.3 Duration of Tail Coverage. Tail coverage shall extend for a period of not less than three (3) / five (5) years from the effective date of termination, or such longer period as may be required by applicable state law or the insurer's standard policy terms.

13.4 Occurrence-Based Policy. If the malpractice insurance policy maintained by Employer is an "occurrence-based" policy, tail coverage shall not be required, and this Section 13.2 through 13.3 shall not apply.

14. Dispute Resolution

14.1 Informal Resolution. The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement through good-faith informal negotiation. The aggrieved Party shall provide written notice to the other Party describing the nature of the dispute. The Parties shall meet within fourteen (14) days of such notice to attempt to resolve the dispute.

14.2 Mediation. If the dispute is not resolved through informal negotiation within thirty (30) days of the initial written notice, the Parties agree to submit the dispute to non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed-upon mediator. The costs of mediation shall be shared equally by the Parties. Mediation shall be conducted in City, State.

14.3 Binding Arbitration. If mediation does not resolve the dispute within sixty (60) days of the initial written notice, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) under its Employment Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with AAA rules and shall take place in City, State. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment upon the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

14.4 Exception for Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm arising from a breach of Sections 7, 8, 9, or 10, without the necessity of posting a bond and without first submitting to mediation or arbitration.

14.5 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of State, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

14.6 Attorneys' Fees. In any action or proceeding to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing Party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees, costs, and expenses from the non-prevailing Party.

15. General Provisions

15.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all exhibits attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, warranties, commitments, offers, and agreements, whether oral or written, relating to Associate's employment.

15.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, legal, and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. 15.4 Waiver. The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such Party's right to enforce that or any other provision in the future. Any waiver must be in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

15.5 Assignment. Associate may not assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without Employer's prior written consent. Employer may assign this Agreement to a successor entity in connection with a sale, merger, or reorganization of the Practice, provided that the successor entity assumes all of Employer's obligations under this Agreement.

15.6 Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed duly given when (a) delivered personally, (b) sent by certified or registered mail, return receipt requested, postage prepaid, (c) sent by overnight courier with confirmed delivery, or (d) sent by email with confirmed receipt, to the addresses set forth in Section 1 or to such other address as a Party may designate in writing.

15.7 Headings. The headings in this Agreement are for convenience of reference only and shall not affect the interpretation or construction of this Agreement.

15.8 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed original signatures for all purposes.

15.9 No Third-Party Beneficiaries. This Agreement is intended solely for the benefit of the Parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns. Nothing in this Agreement shall confer any rights or remedies upon any third party.

15.10 Compliance with Law. Both Parties shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, rules, and regulations in the performance of their obligations under this Agreement.

Insurance Verification Checklist

Complete Workflow for Verifying Chiropractic Benefits Before the First Visit

Insurance verification is one of the most important administrative tasks in your practice. Done well, it prevents billing surprises, builds patient trust, and protects your revenue. Done poorly or skipped, it leads to denied claims, angry patients, and lost income. Verify every new patient before their first visit. No exceptions.

Step 2: Call Workflow

Before You Call

  • Have a copy of the front and back of the insurance card in front of you.
  • Call the provider services number on the back of the card (not the member services number, which is for patients).
  • Have a pen and this checklist ready. You will need to write quickly.
  • Block 10-15 minutes per verification call. Some hold times are long.

Automated System Navigation Tips

  • Most systems will ask for the member ID and date of birth first. Have these ready.
  • When prompted, select the option for "provider" or "provider services," not "member."
  • If the automated system offers eligibility information, listen to it first. It often provides deductible and copay information faster than waiting for a live representative.
  • If you need to speak to a representative, press "0" repeatedly or say "representative" to bypass automated menus on many systems.
  • Many insurers now offer online portals (Availity, NaviNet, the insurer's own website) where you can verify benefits without a phone call. Use these when available to save time.

Common Denials and Prevention Strategies

Understanding the most common denial reasons allows you to prevent them before they happen. Here are the 8 denial codes you will encounter most often in chiropractic billing, and how to avoid them.

Code Denial Reason What It Means Prevention Strategy
CO-4 Procedure code inconsistent with modifier or missing modifier The claim was submitted without a required modifier (typically AT for active/corrective treatment). Always verify modifier requirements during the verification call. Add AT modifier to CMT codes for all active treatment visits. Train your billing team to check modifiers before submission.
CO-18 Duplicate claim/service The insurance company believes this claim was already submitted and processed. Check your billing system for duplicate entries before submitting. If you are resubmitting a corrected claim, use the appropriate frequency code (7 for replacement, 8 for void) to indicate it replaces a prior submission.
CO-29 Time limit for filing has expired The claim was submitted after the payer's filing deadline (typically 90 days to 1 year depending on the payer). Submit claims within 24-48 hours of service. Run a weekly report of unbilled encounters. Set calendar reminders for filing deadlines. If you miss a deadline, appeal with documentation of the delay reason.
CO-50 Non-covered service The specific service or CPT code is not a covered benefit under the patient's plan. Verify covered CPT codes during the benefits verification call. If a code is not covered, inform the patient before providing the service so they can make an informed decision about paying out of pocket.
CO-97 Payment adjusted because the benefit for this service is included in another service performed on the same day You billed two codes that the payer considers bundled. They will pay for one but not the other. Understand NCCI bundling edits. Do not bill E/M codes with CMT codes on the same day without modifier 25 (separately identifiable E/M). Know which therapy codes can and cannot be billed together.
PR-1 Deductible amount The patient's deductible has not been met, so the patient is responsible for the allowed amount. This is not a true denial but a patient responsibility transfer. Verify deductible status before the visit so you can collect from the patient at time of service. Inform the patient of their responsibility upfront.
CO-236 Benefit maximum reached The patient has used all of their covered chiropractic visits for the year. Track visit counts during verification and throughout the care plan. Notify the patient when they are approaching their limit. Transition to a cash or wellness plan for visits beyond the covered limit.
CO-15 Authorization required, not obtained The payer required pre-authorization and it was not obtained before the service was rendered. Always ask about pre-authorization during the verification call. Set up a tracking system for authorization expiration dates. Reauthorize before the current authorization expires. Never assume authorization is automatic.

When to Refer to a Billing Specialist

Not every insurance situation can be handled in-house. Know when to escalate to a professional billing specialist or billing company.

Refer When:

  • You cannot get a clear answer from the insurance company after two attempts. Some plans are deliberately confusing, and a billing specialist has relationships and expertise to cut through the noise.
  • The patient has multiple insurance plans (coordination of benefits). Primary/secondary/tertiary billing rules are complex and errors are costly.
  • You are dealing with a workers' compensation or personal injury case. These require specialized knowledge of lien processes, fee schedules, and legal requirements that differ from standard insurance billing.
  • A claim has been denied multiple times and your internal appeals have failed. A billing specialist can write more effective appeal letters and knows the specific language and documentation insurers respond to.
  • You are consistently seeing denial rates above 10%. This suggests a systemic issue with your billing process that a specialist can diagnose and fix.
  • You are spending more than 10 hours per week on insurance issues. At that point, the cost of a billing specialist is likely less than the cost of the staff time being consumed, and the specialist will likely recover more revenue.

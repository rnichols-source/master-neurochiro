New Patient Onboarding Checklist

Every Step from First Call to Fully Onboarded Patient

New patient onboarding is not a single event. It is a carefully orchestrated sequence of touchpoints designed to build trust, demonstrate competence, and create a patient who is committed to their care plan from day one. When any step is missed, the patient feels it, even if they cannot articulate what went wrong. This checklist ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

Phase 1: Pre-Visit (Before the Patient Arrives)

Timeline: 24-48 hours before Day 1

Task Assigned To Completed
Confirmation call made — Call patient 24-48 hours before appointment. Confirm date, time, location. Remind them to arrive 10 minutes early. Confirm they received paperwork. __________ __________
Confirmation text sent — Send text message confirmation with practice name, address, date, and time. Include a link to online paperwork if available. __________ __________
New patient paperwork sent — Email or mail intake forms, health history, informed consent, HIPAA acknowledgment, and financial policy. Digital submission preferred. __________ __________
Insurance verified — Call insurance company or use online portal. Confirm chiropractic benefits, deductible status, copay/coinsurance, visit limits, and pre-auth requirements. Document reference number and rep name. __________ __________
New patient folder prepped — Create physical or digital folder with: intake forms, insurance verification sheet, financial agreement template, welcome letter, practice brochure, and doctor bio. __________ __________
Team notified — Add new patient to the morning huddle sheet. Note any special circumstances (referral source, specific complaint, VIP referral, language needs). __________ __________

Phase 2: Day 1 (Consultation and Exam)

Timeline: Patient's First Visit

Task Assigned To Completed
Greeted at the door — Stand up, make eye contact, smile, and greet the patient by name before they reach the front desk. First impressions are formed in 7 seconds. __________ __________
Paperwork collected and reviewed — Collect completed intake forms. Review for completeness. Flag any missing fields immediately. Enter data into EHR system. __________ __________
Insurance card copied — Copy front and back of insurance card. Copy photo ID. File in patient folder. __________ __________
Office tour provided — Brief walk-through of the office. Show restrooms, adjusting area, x-ray room, and any relevant treatment areas. Introduce team members by name. __________ __________
Exam room prepared — Clean table, fresh paper, gowns if needed, forms ready for doctor, imaging requisition if applicable. __________ __________
Patient escorted to exam room — Walk the patient to the room (never point). Explain what will happen next. Let them know the doctor will be with them shortly. __________ __________
Doctor consultation and exam completed — Full consultation, history review, orthopedic and neurological exam, posture analysis, and any necessary imaging. __________ __________
Post-exam instructions given — Doctor or CA explains what happens next: "We're going to review all of your findings and prepare a customized care plan for you. We'll go over everything together at your next visit." __________ __________
Day 2 appointment scheduled — Schedule the Report of Findings visit before the patient leaves. Ideally within 24-48 hours. Confirm the date and time verbally and in writing. __________ __________
Patient checked out — Collect any Day 1 fees (exam fee, co-pay). Provide receipt. Hand them a business card and welcome packet. __________ __________

Phase 3: Between Day 1 and Day 2

Timeline: 24-48 Hours Between Visits

Task Assigned To Completed
Doctor reviews all exam data — Complete review of health history, exam findings, imaging, and any lab results. Identify primary and secondary conditions. __________ __________
Care plan built — Determine care plan phases (acute/corrective/wellness), visit frequency, duration, re-exam intervals, and expected outcomes. __________ __________
Financial options prepared — Calculate total care plan cost. Prepare PIF discount, payment plan options, and insurance estimate. Print financial agreement. __________ __________
ROF materials prepared — Print or prepare any visual aids, x-ray comparisons, posture photos, or educational materials needed for the report of findings. __________ __________
Team briefed on patient — During morning huddle, brief the team on the new patient's ROF. Note any sensitivities, communication preferences, or family members who may attend. __________ __________
Confirmation call/text for Day 2 — Confirm the ROF appointment. Remind patient to bring spouse or decision-maker if applicable. __________ __________

Phase 4: Day 2 (Report of Findings)

Timeline: Patient's Second Visit

Task Assigned To Completed
ROF room set up — Consultation room or private area prepared with visual aids, x-rays loaded on screen, care plan printed, financial options printed, pens available. __________ __________
Patient greeted warmly — Welcome patient by name. If spouse or family member is present, introduce yourself and include them in the conversation. __________ __________
Doctor delivers Report of Findings — Present findings clearly using visual aids. Explain the condition, the care plan, expected outcomes, and timeline. Answer all questions. __________ __________
Financial agreement presented — CA or office manager presents financial options clearly. Review PIF discount, payment plan, and insurance coverage. Allow patient to choose. Do not pressure. __________ __________
Financial agreement signed — Patient selects payment option and signs financial agreement. Set up auto-draft if applicable. Collect first payment. __________ __________
Schedule blocked for onboarding — Schedule all visits for the first phase of care. Block out re-exam dates. Provide patient with a printed schedule or digital calendar invites. __________ __________
First adjustment scheduled — If not performed on Day 2, schedule Day 3 (first adjustment) immediately. Ideally same week as ROF. __________ __________

Phase 5: Day 3 (First Adjustment)

Timeline: Patient's Third Visit

Task Assigned To Completed
First adjustment prep — Table and room prepared. Patient's care plan chart accessible. Any special equipment or positioning aids ready. __________ __________
Patient checked in warmly — Greet by name. Ask how they are feeling since the exam. Escort to adjusting area. __________ __________
Doctor performs first adjustment — Explain what you are doing as you do it. Check for comfort. Reassure the patient. Note initial response. __________ __________
Post-adjustment instructions given — Explain what to expect (possible soreness, increased range of motion, etc.). Provide home care instructions (ice/heat, stretches, posture tips). __________ __________
Take-home packet provided — Include: practice brochure, home exercise instructions, what to expect handout, referral cards, and practice business cards. __________ __________
Next 4 visits confirmed — Verify the next 4 appointments are on the schedule. Provide appointment card or text confirmation for all upcoming visits. __________ __________
Referral request planted — "If you know anyone who's dealing with similar issues, we'd love to help them too. Here are a couple of referral cards." __________ __________

Phase 6: Week 1 Follow-Up

Timeline: First 7 Days of Care

Task Assigned To Completed
48-hour courtesy call — Call the patient 48 hours after their first adjustment. Ask how they are feeling. Address any concerns. Reassure about normal post-adjustment responses. Confirm next appointment. __________ __________
Check-in text sent — Send a brief, friendly text: "Hi [Name], just checking in after your first adjustment. How are you feeling? Let us know if you have any questions. We're here for you!" __________ __________
Progress noted in chart — Document patient's response to first adjustment, any feedback from follow-up call, and any adjustments needed to the care plan. __________ __________
Google review request sent — After confirming a positive experience, send a friendly request for an online review with a direct link to your Google Business page. __________ __________
Welcome email/letter sent — A personal note from the doctor welcoming the patient to the practice, reinforcing the care plan, and expressing genuine excitement about their health journey. __________ __________
Referral source thanked — If the patient was referred, send a thank-you note or call to the referring party within 48 hours. __________ __________
Onboarding checklist filed — Completed checklist filed in patient folder for reference. Note any incomplete items and assign follow-up. __________ __________

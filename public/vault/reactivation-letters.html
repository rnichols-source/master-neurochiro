Patient Reactivation Letters

How to Use These Letters

These three letters form a complete reactivation sequence designed to bring inactive patients back into your practice. Each letter targets a different stage of inactivity and uses a distinct psychological approach. Print each letter on your practice letterhead, fill in the personalized fields, and mail in a hand-addressed envelope for maximum open rates. Consider including a business card with each letter.

Timing: Send Letter 1 at 30 days inactive, Letter 2 at 90 days inactive, and Letter 3 at 6 months or more of inactivity. Track all mailings in your practice management system.

Implementation Guide

Best Practices for Maximum Response

  • Hand-address the envelopes. Hand-written addresses have significantly higher open rates than printed labels. If volume is too high, use a handwriting-style font printed directly on the envelope.
  • Use a real stamp. Metered mail looks like bulk mail. A real stamp signals a personal letter.
  • Print on quality paper. Use your practice letterhead on premium paper stock. The letter should feel substantial and professional.
  • Personalize every field. Never send a letter with blank fill-in fields. Take the time to reference the patient's actual condition, care plan, or last visit. This personal touch is what separates an effective reactivation letter from junk mail.
  • Track your results. Note in each patient's file when each letter was sent. Track who responds, who schedules, and who returns to care. This data will help you refine your approach over time.
  • Follow up by phone. For your highest-value patients, consider following up with a personal phone call 5-7 days after the letter is mailed. The combination of a letter and a call dramatically increases response rates.
  • Be consistent. Run this reactivation sequence every month. Pull your inactive patient list on the first of each month and send the appropriate letter based on how long each patient has been inactive.

What to Expect on Your First Visit

Your first visit is designed to be thorough and unhurried. Please plan to spend approximately 45 to 60 minutes with us so we can give you the attention you deserve.

Step 1: Arrival and Check-In (5-10 minutes)

Please arrive 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time. Our front desk team will greet you, collect your completed paperwork, verify your insurance information, and answer any last-minute questions. We want you to feel comfortable and welcome from the moment you walk through our door.

Step 2: One-on-One Consultation (10-15 minutes)

You will sit down privately with the doctor for a detailed conversation about your health. We will discuss your primary concern, your health history, your goals for care, and what you hope to achieve. This is your time to tell us everything. No detail is too small, and no question is too basic.

Step 3: Comprehensive Examination (15-20 minutes)

The doctor will perform a thorough chiropractic and neurological examination. This may include postural analysis, range of motion testing, orthopedic tests, neurological assessments, and spinal palpation. Depending on your condition, we may also take digital X-rays to get a clear picture of your spinal structure.

Step 4: Report of Findings (10-15 minutes)

The doctor will explain exactly what was found during your examination. Using your X-rays and exam results, we will show you where the problems are, what is causing your symptoms, and most importantly, what we can do about it. You will receive a clear, customized care plan with specific recommendations.

Step 5: Your First Adjustment (5-10 minutes)

If clinically appropriate, you may receive your first chiropractic adjustment on this visit. The doctor will explain every step before it happens. You will never be surprised. Many patients report feeling immediate relief, though individual results vary.

Step 6: Checkout and Scheduling (5 minutes)

Our team will review your care plan schedule, process your payment or insurance, and book your next appointments. We will also provide you with any at-home care instructions, exercises, or recommendations.

Informed Consent for Chiropractic Care

Nature of Chiropractic Care

Chiropractic care involves the assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of conditions related to the spine, nervous system, and musculoskeletal system. The primary treatment method used by chiropractors is spinal adjustment or manipulation, which involves the application of a controlled force to a specific joint to restore proper motion and function.

Potential Benefits

The potential benefits of chiropractic care may include, but are not limited to:

  • Reduction or elimination of pain and discomfort
  • Improved joint mobility and range of motion
  • Decreased muscle tension and spasm
  • Improved nervous system function
  • Increased flexibility and physical performance
  • Improved posture
  • Enhanced overall health and well-being
  • Reduced need for medications or surgical interventions

Potential Risks and Complications

As with any healthcare treatment, there are potential risks associated with chiropractic care, although complications are rare. These may include:

  • Short-term soreness, stiffness, or aching at the site of treatment (most common, typically resolves within 24-48 hours)
  • Temporary increase in existing symptoms
  • Fatigue or tiredness following treatment
  • Headache
  • In rare cases: disc injury, strain or sprain of a ligament or muscle, rib fracture
  • In extremely rare cases (estimated at 1 in 1-2 million adjustments): stroke or vertebral artery injury associated with cervical (neck) adjustment

Alternatives to Chiropractic Care

Alternatives to chiropractic care may include:

  • Self-administered over-the-counter pain medications
  • Prescription medications from a medical doctor
  • Physical therapy
  • Massage therapy
  • Acupuncture
  • Surgical intervention
  • No treatment (accepting the natural progression of the condition)

Your Rights as a Patient

You have the right to:

  • Ask questions about any aspect of your care at any time
  • Refuse or discontinue any treatment at any time without prejudice
  • Request a modification of any treatment
  • Seek a second opinion from another healthcare provider
  • Be informed of your diagnosis, treatment plan, and prognosis

Payment Expectations

Payment is due at the time of service. We accept cash, personal checks, Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover, and Health Savings Account (HSA) and Flexible Spending Account (FSA) cards. For your convenience, we also offer the ability to keep a card on file for automatic payment.

Insurance Filing

As a courtesy, we will file claims with your insurance company on your behalf. However, please understand that:

  • Your insurance policy is a contract between you and your insurance company. We are not a party to that contract.
  • You are ultimately responsible for all charges regardless of your insurance coverage or the outcome of any claim.
  • We will verify your insurance benefits before your first visit, but verification is not a guarantee of payment. Your insurance company makes all final coverage decisions.
  • Your estimated co-pay, co-insurance, and deductible amounts are due at each visit.
  • If your insurance company denies a claim or fails to pay within 60 days, the balance becomes your responsibility.
  • It is your responsibility to notify us of any changes to your insurance coverage.

Uninsured and Self-Pay Patients

For patients without insurance or who choose to pay out-of-pocket, we offer competitive self-pay rates. Please ask our front desk team about our current fee schedule and any available care packages.

Payment Plans

  • Care Plan Packages: Pre-paid care packages that offer a reduced per-visit rate when purchased in advance.

# How to Structure Chiropractic Care Plans for Maximum Retention

One of the biggest mistakes chiropractors make is offering "pay as you go" care. This creates a transactional relationship with your patients. In the **NeuroChiro Mastermind**, we teach a **care plan architecture** that prioritizes clinical results and patient retention.

If your patients drop out after their initial symptoms are gone, you have a care plan structure problem.

## 1. The 3-Phases of Care
We recommend structuring your care plans into three distinct phases:
1. **The Intensive Phase:** (Usually 4-12 weeks) - This is where the initial reorganization and stabilization happen. High frequency is key.
2. **The Corrective Phase:** (Usually 3-6 months) - This is where we reinforce the new neurological patterns and build clinical stability.
3. **The Wellness Phase:** (Ongoing) - This is the long-term maintenance of a healthy, regulated nervous system.

## 2. Why "Pay as You Go" Fails
When patients pay by the visit, they are deciding every time whether they "need" an adjustment based on how they feel. This leads to inconsistent care and poor clinical outcomes. 

**The Fix:** Transition to **all-inclusive care plan packages.** This shifts the focus from the cost per visit to the value of the result.

## 3. Communication is Key
You must explain the *why* behind your care plan on Day 2 (ROF). If the patient doesn't understand that it takes time to retrain the nervous system, they will stop care as soon as their pain is gone.

**The Strategy:** Use the **NeuroChiro Care Plan Script.** Explain that we aren't just treating a symptom; we are reorganizing their ability to adapt to stress.

## 4. The 12-Month Wellness Model
The most successful NeuroChiro practices transition their patients into 12-month wellness memberships after their initial corrective phase. This creates predictable recurring revenue for the clinic and long-term health for the patient.

# 5 Chiropractic Communication Mistakes Killing Your Case Acceptance

In the **NeuroChiro Mastermind**, we analyze hundreds of patient interactions. Most "sales problems" in a chiropractic office are actually **leadership and communication problems.**

If you find yourself constantly hearing "I need to think about it" or "I'll call you back," you are likely making one of these five critical errors.

## 1. Over-Educating with "Big Words"
Patients don't care about your Latin terminology or the complex biomechanics of the C1-C2 articulation. They care about their life. When you over-explain, the patient's brain enters a state of "cognitive load" and shuts down.

**The Fix:** Use the **NeuroChiro Neural Bridge.** Speak in terms of adaptation, stress regulation, and life quality.

## 2. Asking for Permission Instead of Leading
"Would you like to start care today?" is a weak question. You are the clinical authority. You have identified a problem in their nervous system that requires correction. 

**The Fix:** Transition to leadership. "Based on your scans, we need to begin your initial phase of care today to stabilize this pattern. Let's head to the front to finalize the schedule."

## 3. Defending the Price
The moment you start justifying your fees, you have lost the value proposition. Price is only an issue in the absence of value.

**The Fix:** Focus on the **ROI of their health.** Show them what it's costing them *not* to get adjusted (missed work, poor sleep, inability to play with their kids).

## 4. Ignoring the "Subconscious Objection"
Patients often have a hidden objection they won't tell you. Usually, it's a lack of trust in the process or a previous bad experience with another provider.

**The Fix:** Address the elephant in the room. Ask: "Is there anything about the scans or the care plan we've discussed that doesn't make total sense to you yet?"

## 5. Lack of Consistency Between Day 1 and Day 2
If your energy is high on Day 1 (the exam) but you seem nervous on Day 2 (the money talk), the patient will sense the incongruence and back away.

**The Fix:** Standardize your **Clinical OS.** Your Day 2 should be a natural, high-certainty extension of your Day 1 findings.

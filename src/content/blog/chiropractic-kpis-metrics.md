# The 5 Chiropractic KPIs You Need to Track Every Week

Most chiropractors "manage by chaos." They look at their bank account at the end of the month and wonder why they aren't growing. In the **NeuroChiro Mastermind**, we teach that **what you measure, you can manage.**

If you want a stable, predictable, and profitable clinic, you must track these five Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) every week.

## 1. PVA (Patient Visit Average)
PVA is the most critical metric for long-term stability. It tells you how long a patient stays in your care. A low PVA means you are constantly on the "new patient treadmill."

**The Goal:** A healthy, neurologically focused practice should aim for a PVA that reflects full initial care plan completion (usually 24-48 visits).

## 2. Case Average (Revenue Per Case)
How much revenue does each new patient represent over the course of their care? If your case average is too low, you are working too hard for too little reward.

**The Strategy:** Transition to high-integrity, multi-month care plans that reflect the actual time it takes to reorganize a nervous system.

## 3. PVA / Case Acceptance Ratio
How many of your new patients actually commit to your recommended care? If you have 10 new patients but only 2 start care, you have a **Communication Problem**, not a marketing problem.

**The Fix:** Audit your **Report of Findings architecture.** Ensure your value proposition is clear and your clinical certainty is high.

## 4. Re-Sign Rate
What percentage of patients who complete their initial care plan transition into long-term wellness or maintenance care? This is the ultimate metric for patient education effectiveness.

**The Fix:** Start the "wellness conversation" on Day 1, not on their last visit.

## 5. Overhead %
A high-volume clinic can still go broke if the overhead is out of control. Your overhead should leave you with a healthy profit margin to reinvest in yourself and your team.

**The Goal:** Aim for an overhead of 40-50% for a lean, high-authority practice.

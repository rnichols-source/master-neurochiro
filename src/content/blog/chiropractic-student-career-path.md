# Associate vs. Owner: What's the Best Path After Chiropractic School?

The transition from chiropractic student to practicing doctor is one of the most critical phases of your career. In the **NeuroChiro Mastermind**, we work with hundreds of students to help them make this choice with clarity and certainty.

There is no "right" answer, but there is a right answer for **you.**

## Option 1: The Associate Path
Becoming an associate in a high-volume, high-authority clinic is the fastest way to master your clinical skills. You get to learn on someone else's dime while getting your hands on patients every day.

**Pros:**
* Immediate income (usually).
* Mentorship from an experienced doctor.
* No overhead or marketing stress.
* Mastery of adjusting and communication.

**Cons:**
* Lower income ceiling.
* Lack of autonomy in business decisions.
* You are building someone else's asset.

## Option 2: Opening Your Own Clinic
Starting your own practice from scratch (or buying an existing one) gives you total freedom and total responsibility.

**Pros:**
* Total autonomy.
* You own the equity in your business.
* No cap on your income.
* You can build the exact culture you want.

**Cons:**
* High initial risk and debt.
* You are responsible for marketing, billing, and hiring.
* Stress of being a business owner while also being a doctor.

## The "NeuroChiro Way"
At NeuroChiro, we believe that whether you associate or open, you must possess **Clinical Authority.** If you open your own clinic but can't communicate value, you will struggle. If you associate but don't understand the business metrics, you will never be fairly compensated.

## How to Choose?
Ask yourself these three questions:
1. Is my clinical adjusting skill at a level where I could lead a patient through a 12-month care plan today?
2. Do I have a proven system for patient communication (ROF)?
3. Do I have the capital or the risk tolerance to go 6 months without a paycheck?

If the answer to any of those is "no," an associateship with a **NeuroChiro-approved mentor** is likely your best move.

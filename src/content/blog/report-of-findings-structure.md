# The Report of Findings (ROF) Architecture

Most chiropractors struggle with the Report of Findings because they treat it like a sales pitch. They try to convince the patient that they need care, which creates immediate resistance.

In the NeuroChiro Mastermind, we teach a different approach: **Clinical Authority.**

## 1. The Mindset Shift
The goal of the ROF is not to sell. The goal is to provide the patient with so much clinical certainty that choosing anything other than care seems illogical. You are the expert. You are presenting the data of their nervous system.

## 2. The 3 Questions Every Patient Has
Before a patient will commit to a care plan, they need answers to three specific questions:
1. Can you help me?
2. How long will it take?
3. How much will it cost?

If you don't answer these clearly and confidently, you will lose them.

## 3. Using Objective Data
Stop over-explaining philosophy on Day 2. Use the scans. Use the data. Show them where their nervous system is struggling and what that means for their long-term health.

## 4. The Recommendation
Your recommendation should be based on clinical necessity, not their insurance coverage or what you think they can afford. When you lower your clinical standards to match a patient's perceived budget, you do a disservice to both of you.

## Conclusion
The ROF is the most important 15 minutes in your clinic. Master the architecture, and you will see your case acceptance skyrocket.

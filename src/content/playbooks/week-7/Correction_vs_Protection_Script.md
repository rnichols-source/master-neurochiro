# Correction vs. Protection Script: The Long-Term Care Transition

The goal is to transition the patient from "fixing a problem" (Correction) to "protecting the reorganization" (Protection).

## 1. Defining the Difference
*   **Correction:** *"We've spent the last 12 weeks actively reorganizing the way your nervous system handles stress. We've seen [Specific Scan Result] shift from here to here. We have successfully changed the baseline pattern."*

*   **The Transition:** *"Now that the pattern has changed, we are moving from the **Correction Phase** into the **Protection Phase**."*

## 2. The Analogy (Use one)
*   **The Gym:** *"It's like spending 6 months in the gym to get in shape. You don't get 'fixed' and then quit forever. You protect the results you worked for."*
*   **The Teeth:** *"It's like getting your teeth cleaned. You don't wait for a cavity to show up again to visit the dentist. You protect the health of the system."*

## 3. The Logic
*   **The Strategy:** *"Life is still happening. You're still working at a desk, you're still sleeping 6 hours some nights, and you still have stress. If we don't protect the new pattern, your nervous system will naturally drift back toward the old one. We want to prevent that."*

## 4. The Recommendation
*   **The Script:** *"To protect the progress we've made, my recommendation is to move into a wellness frequency of [1x per week / 2x per month]. This allows us to keep the system regulated and catch any stress before it creates a new pattern."*

**The Operator Mantra:** *"Correction is about change. Protection is about certainty."*

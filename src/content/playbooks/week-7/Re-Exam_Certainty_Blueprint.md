# Re-Exam Certainty Blueprint: The Perspective Reset

A re-exam is not just a clinical check-in. It is a **Structural Authority Anchor.**

## 1. Resetting Perspective
Human beings quickly forget their baseline. They recalibrate to feeling better and start comparing their results to "perfect" instead of "where I started."

**The Goal:** Show the distance traveled. Remind them of the problem they came in to solve.

## 2. The Architecture of a Re-Exam
1.  **Acknowledge Progress:** Start with what has objectively changed.
2.  **Highlight the Baseline:** Remind them of the original scan or exam findings.
3.  **Identify Remaining Layers:** Show them where the system is still adapting.
4.  **Confirm the Next Phase:** Explain the logic of the continuing care plan.

## 3. The Certainty Reset
If you don't anchor progress, the patient will fill in the blanks with doubt.
*   **Don't say:** *"You're doing good, keep it up."* (Weak)
*   **Do say:** *"Look at the shift in your scan from Week 1 to now. We've stabilized the primary stressor, but we still have these two secondary patterns to reorganize. This is why we're moving into Phase 2."* (Strong)

## 4. Stability Over Motivation
Don't rely on the patient "valuing their health." Rely on the system showing them the value. A strong re-exam makes the next 3 months of care make perfect sense.

**The Blueprint:** Re-exams are the anchors that prevent behavioral drift.

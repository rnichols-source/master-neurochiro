# The Containment Protocol Cheat Sheet

Protect your clinical authority during moments of uncertainty.

## 1. Redefining the Objection
An objection is not a challenge to your authority. It is **Uncertainty Expressed Out Loud.** The patient’s nervous system is tightening because it feels a lack of safety or predictability.

## 2. The Power of Silence
Silence is the ultimate clinical leverage. It gives the patient's nervous system room to regulate.
*   **The Silence Rule:** When you speak more, the room destabilizes. When you speak less, the room stabilizes.
*   **Containment vs. Persuasion:** Stop trying to move the patient. Just hold the room steady.

## 3. Identity Detachment
Your clinical value is not tied to their "yes." When you detach your identity from their agreement, you stop being reactive.
*   **Reactivity:** Explaining more, defending your plan, or softening policies.
*   **Containment:** Calm tone, clear expectations, and a steady posture.

## 4. The Response Architecture
When a patient objects or questions care:
1.  **Stop Talking:** Do not immediately counter.
2.  **Observe:** Watch their nervous system state.
3.  **Hold the Structure:** Restate the clinical necessity without emotion or pressure.
4.  **Allow space:** Let the patient fill the silence.

**Conclusion:** Authority is established not by your arguments, but by your stability.

# The Emotional Cycle Map: Predicting Patient Behavior

Patients do not stay emotionally consistent forever. They move through predictable phases. If you understand the phase, you stop reacting to the behavior.

## 1. Hope (Week 1-2)
*   **The State:** High motivation, excitement, optimism.
*   **The Risk:** High expectations for immediate "cures."
*   **The Operator Action:** Ground them in the reality of the 8-12 week reorganization phase.

## 2. Relief (Week 3-6)
*   **The State:** Symptoms drop, function improves.
*   **The Risk:** The patient assumes they are "done."
*   **The Operator Action:** Reconnect them to the baseline scans. Remind them relief is the first step, not the goal.

## 3. Doubt (Week 6-10)
*   **The State:** Plateauing results, curiosity about frequency. "Do I still need this?"
*   **The Risk:** Behavioral drift. Missing visits.
*   **The Operator Action:** This is where you perform the **Re-Exam Authority Anchor.** Show objective progress.

## 4. Testing (Ongoing)
*   **The State:** Stretching visits to see if the structure holds.
*   **The Risk:** Trust erosion if the doctor panics or softens.
*   **The Operator Action:** **Containment Protocol.** Stay steady. Maintain the structure.

## 5. Recommitment or Drift
*   **The Result:** If the structure held, they settle into long-term wellness. If the structure was weak, they drift away until the next symptom spike.

**The Operator Mantra:** *"I don't chase their hope, and I don't fear their doubt. I manage the system."*

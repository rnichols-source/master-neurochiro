# The Flare-Up Protocol: Structural Stability During Setbacks

A flare-up is not an emergency. It is **Clinical Information.**

## 1. The Doctor's Reaction is the Primary Problem
When a patient reports a flare-up, the stability of the entire system depends on your reaction.
*   **Panic:** Fast speech, excessive explaining, changing frequencies too quickly. (Weak)
*   **Containment:** Calm tone, non-reactive posture, steady clinical certainty. (Strong)

## 2. Redefining the Flare-Up
A flare-up is a sign that the system isn't fully stable yet or that stress exceeded the current capacity.
*   **Not a Failure:** It doesn't mean care stopped working.
*   **Not a Regression:** It means the pattern is still being reorganized.

## 3. Healing Waves
Remind the patient: *"Healing isn't a straight line. It moves in waves. This spike just shows us where the system is still adapting."*

## 4. The Protocol
1.  **Don't Apologize:** You didn't break their back; life did.
2.  **Stay Calm:** If you lose certainty, the patient loses certainty.
3.  **Reinforce the Structure:** Don't suddenly abandon the care plan. Stick to the OS.
4.  **Observe the Pattern:** Use the spike to learn where the nervous system is still vulnerable.

**Conclusion:** Authority is strengthened when the doctor remains steady during a setback.

# Week 7: Patient Behavior & Population Thinking

## Phase Overview
Week 7 is about moving from "Patient Thinking" to "Population Thinking." It is the shift from emotionally reacting to every individual situation to structurally managing the clinic based on patterns of human behavior.

## Core Objectives
1.  **Shift to Population Thinking:** Stop taking missed visits, flare-ups, and doubt personally.
2.  **Master the Containment Protocol:** Use non-reactive posture and silence to stabilize the clinical environment.
3.  **Use Re-Exams as Authority Anchors:** Reset patient perspective and stabilize long-term retention.
4.  **Handle Flare-Ups without Structural Collapse:** Maintain certainty even when symptoms spike.
5.  **Understand the Emotional Cycle:** Predict and manage hope, relief, doubt, and testing.

## Training Units
*   **7.1: The Reality of Patient Behavior:** Why school didn't prepare you for human messy patterns.
*   **7.2: Missed Visits & Structural Containment:** Handling cancellations without destabilizing the system.
*   **7.3: Re-Exams as Authority Anchors:** Using progress reviews as perspective resets.
*   **7.4: Symptom Flare-Ups:** Why setbacks test your systems, not your clinical skill.
*   **7.5: Correction vs. Protection:** Transitioning into the long-term care conversation.
*   **7.6: The Emotional Cycle of Patients:** Mastering the phases of the patient journey.

## Critical Assets for This Week
1.  **The Containment Protocol:** Protecting clinical authority during uncertainty.
2.  **Population Thinking Playbook:** Managing your clinic like an operator.
3.  **Re-Exam Certainty Blueprint:** The exact structure for progress reviews.
4.  **The Flare-Up Protocol:** Staying calm when symptoms spike.
5.  **Correction vs. Protection Script:** Transitions for long-term care.
